Designing with details

Dan Staffer

Video

Dan Staffer's lecture was about websites and all the different microinteractions that take place. A microinteraction is just one single interaction or task. Some examples of microinteractions are

  • setting your alarm
  • liking a post on facebook
  • pick a password

Microinteractions control an experience with a device or website. Microinteractions are good for accomplishing a task, connecting devices, adjusting settings and many more. We usually dont think about microinteractions but they are so essential to our everyday lives. He used an example of a man that was at the orchestra and his phone wouldnt stop ringing even though it was on silent. The man thought if he turned his sound off that meant all the sounds would stop, but that is not the case, the alarms will still make a sound. This microinteraction that he did not know about was kind of a big deal. Microinteractions may seem so small but it is how it makes our lives easier and helps us out throughout our day to day lives.

Fun Facts

  • Microinteractions can increase loyalty to a product
  • There are 4 stages of microinteractions
  • There is a trigger, rules, feedback and than loops and modes

Dont fear the internet

Russ Maschmeyer

Video

In this 3 part series Russ talked about what the internet is and how to build a webpage. The internet is a series of connected computers and servers. When we type in a website it is just code for an IP address. Anyone can visit a website, but it takes special access to be allowed to change a website. There is two types of websites, a static website and a dynamic website. A static website is pretty basic, it has a folder of pages written in html. Htnl is a structure for a website. Css is what is used to make a html website look pretty. Using an external css page makes it easier to have all of your designs in one area, it makes it so you do not have to keep repeating styles over and over.
